Datapads and Communications

The average citizen interacts with data through a standard datapad, a low-res screen with dials and buttons for navigation (if you're lucky) but more often than not just a screen for reading information. Think of this as a discrete piece of hardware designed strictly to store, read, and edit local files. There are no native wireless transceivers or long-range holo-projectors built in. If your character wants to make a wireless comm call or project a hologram, they have to physically attach dedicated hardware modules. Those are usually heavy extra pieces of machinery you need to lug around. The reliance on physical hardware is driven by strategic necessity. High-energy plasma weapons, ion weapons, and environmental radiation can easily disrupt wireless signals. Because of this, information is stored physically on mediums like datacards, credit chips, and code cylinders, all of which must be physically transferred from person to person.

Slicing and Security

System security relies heavily on physical isolation. Remote hacking is virtually non-existent here. A slicer cannot disable a security grid or download data from a distant location. They have to physically infiltrate the facility, locate a specific terminal, and establish a cable connection. Slicing resembles physical lock-picking much more than coding. To bypass secure mainframes, slicers use Computer Spikes. A spike is a specialized hardware tool packed with high-density logic gates that forces a hardware level override. Because the intense data feedback usually burns out their innards, spikes are strictly single-use consumables.

 

The HoloNet

Interstellar communication is highly restricted, heavily regulated, and technologically demanding. The HoloNet relies on a system of specialized hyperwave satellites aligned along major hyperspace routes. It is primarily utilized for government messaging, military coordination, and high-priority corporate communications. The physical limitations are severe. The connection gets incredibly unstable and outrageously expensive the further away from the Core you travel. It takes time for data to be transferred, and is often outpaced by simple couriers. While you can access the HoloNet from large dish, datapads and comms do not have that level of access.

Combat: Personal Shields and the Humble Knife

Combat is defined by the stalemate between energy weapons and personal shielding. Personal Defense Fields are specifically calibrated to intercept and disperse high-velocity plasma, blaster bolts, fire, and localized cold. Physical projectiles, kinetic impacts, and sonic vibrations pass entirely through them. Because shields are so effective at neutralizing blasters, the galaxy has seen a huge resurgence in advanced melee weapons like the vibroblade and simple combat knives. Shields also have strict operational limits. They come configured with a standard capacity of five internal charges. An activation lasts for the duration of a single combat engagement. After that, the shield shuts down to prevent an explosion or a lethal dose of radiation.

Astrogation and Hyperspace

All the stellar bodies in the galaxy are constantly moving, which causes hyperlanes to drift. To plot a safe trajectory, a starship navicomputer must be updated daily with astronomical data received via localized port connections. Navigating without current charts drastically increases the risk of slamming face first into a planet or star. Hyperdrives are classified by number, and the smaller the number, the faster your hyperdrive. Military vessels are typically equipped with high-speed class 2 or class 3 hyperdrives. Civilian freighters usually scrape by with much slower class 9 to class 16 units. True class 1 hyperdrives are extremely rare.

Medical Tech and Kolto

Medical tech in this era is defined by an over-reliance on Kolto. This naturally occurring liquid compound accelerates cellular regeneration and allows injured organic tissue to heal rapidly. Because it has to be harvested directly from one specific area on Manaan, the Selkath hold immense geopolitical leverage and enforce strict neutrality. Localized medical bundles and synthetic alternatives exist, but they are far worse at treating grievous wounds. Bacta exists but is an inefficient, overly expensive, and not widespread. Most major factions rely on Kolto exclusively.
