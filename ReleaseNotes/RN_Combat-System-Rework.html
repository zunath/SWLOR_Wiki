Design Bible: Combat Upgrade Design Bible

Contributors: John Republic, Girlfailure, jax, Ryudo, AsuraKing, flyingaces, forsakenreaver, Hazin, pastius, Announcer, Witch Time

 

This is a plain-English preview of the upcoming combat upgrade. These changes are not live yet. The exact release date and NWSync hash will be added when the update is ready to deploy.

Quick Summary

  • Combat is being rebuilt from the ground up so weapons, Force powers, Devices, Leadership, First Aid, beasts, defenses, and enemy weaknesses all fit together better.
  • Every character will get a required full rebuild the first time they log in after the update.
  • You will not need a rebuild token. The server will send you into the rebuild flow automatically.
  • Old or removed combat perks will be refunded where needed before your character is rebuilt.
  • Most combat trees are being trimmed down to fewer hotbar buttons, with more of the extra bonuses moved into passive perks.
  • Armor is still an active skill and now counts toward the new 400 total skill rank cap.
  • The update also includes clearer ability text, new Player Guide pages, combat feedback polish, droid equipment updates, name display improvements, and disguise support.
  • New this release: a Mimicry skill line for copying creature techniques, per-skill mastery (capstone) quests, DM-free lightsaber construction with Kyber Tokens, and a player-run Quest Contracts board.
  • Quality-of-life additions include scheduled shuttle travel, globally accessible banks, a portrait Mini-Vitals overlay, and a redesigned Perks window.

What Is Changing

  • The old combat setup is being replaced. Most combat perks, abilities, stats, status effects, item requirements, and enemies are changing.
  • Many old broad perk groups are split into clearer combat trees. For example, ranged combat becomes Pistol, Rifle, and Throwing, and Force is split into Light, Dark, and Universal lines.
  • Many current active buttons are removed, replaced, or folded into passives. Expect to rebuild your hotbars after the update.
  • Shared cooldown groups are shown in the Perks window, so it should be easier to tell which abilities block each other.
  • Area attacks and delayed attacks use clearer warning shapes. Several line and area attacks also check line of sight more carefully.
  • NPCs and creatures are rebalanced for the new combat rules, with new enemy abilities, new enemy variants, and more reliable leashing and chase behavior.
  • The in-game Player Guide explains several of the new systems in plain language.
  • The update also includes the new /name system, so unknown characters behave differently.
  • Recognized names, unknown descriptions, and disguises display more consistently across chat, HoloNet-style messages, staff tools, property lists, and ship lists.

What Happens When You Log In

  • Your character will be sent to the rebuild area.
  • You will rebuild your stats, skills, and perks under the new combat rules.
  • The rebuild is required for everyone because many combat perks and item requirements are changing.
  • Deleted combat perks and obsolete combat instruction items are cleaned up so they do not remain stuck on old characters, beasts, droids, storage, markets, ships, or outfits.
  • Old combat feat buttons are also removed when your character is updated, so old versions should not stay stuck in your feat list.
  • Old item data is updated behind the scenes so existing weapons, armor, enhancements, droids, and stored items use the new combat values.

Character Building

  • The total skill rank cap is now 400.
  • Armor counts toward that cap and grants skill points (SP) like other active skills.
  • Equipment requirements now care more about your skill ranks instead of old proficiency-style perk purchases.
  • Higher ranks of an active ability replace lower ranks, so your feat list and hotbar should have fewer duplicate versions of the same power.
  • Many former active perks are now passive bonuses, upgrades, or riders on other abilities. This should make builds less crowded without removing the identity of each tree.
  • Combat Readiness is replacing the old cooldown-reduction bonus. It improves activated ability damage and healing instead of reducing cooldowns.
  • The Character Sheet and rebuild screens will show skill point numbers more clearly and should better match your build.
  • Ability descriptions will show shared recast groups when abilities use the same cooldown timer.

Combat Feel

  • Damage, hit chance, evasion, critical hits, defense, and scaling have all been rebalanced.
  • Most combat trees should have a clearer rhythm: fewer buttons, stronger identity, and less pressure to fill every hotbar slot.
  • Several area attacks now show warning shapes before they land, such as lines, cones, or circles.
  • If an ability has a cast time, moving or interrupting the action can cancel the targeting preview.
  • Line attacks and other area attacks will check line of sight more reliably.
  • Weapon speeds have been adjusted so fast weapons still feel fast while leaving room for haste and speed bonuses to matter.
  • Some enemies now pressure specific defenses or weaknesses, so swapping gear, using support abilities, and planning around resistances should matter more.
  • Enemy leashing and chase behavior is tighter, including ranged enemies, so enemies should be less likely to fight you from the wrong area or keep attacking when they cannot properly reach you.
  • Ability animations, sounds, warning shapes, and impact feedback are clearer, so combat should be easier to read while it is happening.

Defenses And Resistances

  • Physical Defense and Force Defense are now shown separately from Resistances.
  • Resistances are easier to read: positive values reduce that kind of damage or status effect, while negative values mean you are vulnerable to it.
  • A Resistance of 100 means temporary immunity, but normal gear and passive bonuses will not casually reach full immunity.
  • Elemental damage now respects your main defense first, then applies the matching Resistance afterward.
  • Trauma and Disruption are mainly about resisting matching status effects, not reducing every kind of normal damage.
  • Attack Deflection, Shield Deflection, and Guard are separate defensive tools. Deflection stops a hit from landing; Guard reduces damage after a hit gets through.

Status Effects

  • Many buffs and debuffs have been cleaned up, renamed, or rebuilt so their behavior is more consistent.
  • Expect familiar combat conditions such as Blind, Dazed, Stunned, Knockdown, Hamstring, Sunder, Exposed, Hemorrhage, Toxin, Weakened, and Exhausted to matter under the new system.
  • Damage-over-time effects such as Bleed and Toxin now scale in a more consistent way.
  • Some debuffs now have clearer jobs. For example, Exposed lowers defense, Hemorrhage makes targets take more damage, Fractured Focus makes Force powers more expensive, and Foggy Mind makes activation slower.
  • Several stances, marks, challenges, guards, and auras now trigger extra effects when you attack, defend, guard, deflect, or support allies.
  • Area and status powers such as Creeping Terror use clearer area size and visual feedback.

Weapon Skill Changes

Every weapon skill is being updated. The short version is that each one should have a clearer playstyle and fewer unnecessary active buttons.

Several individual weapon abilities also have clearer feedback, including Vibroknife telegraphs and sound feedback, Staff Crusher area effects, and Rifle targeting and impact animation behavior.

Force, Devices, Leadership, And First Aid

Mimicry (New Skill Line)

  • Mimicry is a new combat skill that lets you copy and use the techniques enemy creatures fight with.
  • You are introduced to it through the intro quest "Salvaged Intelligence" from Renna Tarsk in the Veles Colony, which points you to the Combat Analyzer perk and the /techniques window.
  • Once you own the Combat Analyzer, your analyzer records techniques used by nearby enemies. When one of those enemies dies near you, you have a chance to permanently learn its technique. Your learn chance rises with your Mimicry rank and the Pattern Recognition perk.
  • Manage your library and build a loadout in the /techniques window, which can only be changed out of combat. Equipped actives go on your hotbar, while equipped stances and passive traits apply automatically.
  • Techniques cost Stamina instead of Force Points and work the same for every character type. They come in four tiers unlocked at Mimicry ranks 0, 15, 30, and 45, and cover damage, control, debuffs, ally support, stances, and passive traits.
  • You have a limited number of technique slots that grow as you invest in the skill, so you tailor your loadout to each fight. Equipping several techniques of the same damage element grants a resonance bonus, and the Overclocked Analyzer capstone adds more slots and a burst ability.

Skill Mastery Quests

  • Each combat and support skill now has capstone "mastery" quest chains for characters who reach rank 50 in that skill.
  • Every skill has two mastery paths, one for each of its combat styles, and each path is a five-stage chain that builds from clearing groups of trial enemies to defeating a final master.
  • The trials take place in dedicated locations across the galaxy, such as the Jedi Enclave Trial Halls on Dantooine and the Czerka Arms Test Range on Nar Shaddaa.
  • The later stages are built for a group, so expect to bring companions or allies for the wardens and masters.
  • Completing a mastery chain awards XP, credits, and a matching achievement.

Beast Mastery

  • Beast Mastery is being updated across taming, training, rewards, soothing, revives, beast attacks, beast roles, and beast support abilities.
  • Beast stats, levels, purity bonuses, resistances, and attack speed behavior are being adjusted to match the new combat rules.
  • Tame will care about your social stats in a clearer way.
  • Beast builds should have clearer roles, including bruiser, damage, evasion, Force, tank, balanced, training, and bioengineer support.
  • New Incubation Field Notes are codex key items, one per mutation-target beast, that spell out every way to incubate that beast and what each method needs, such as the required enzyme, day of week, and minimum success chance.
  • You earn a beast's note automatically the first time you successfully incubate it. Starter notes are also sold by Zoya the Beastwright in the Veles Colony, and some are dropped by specific world bosses.

Items, Crafting, And Enemies

  • Combat-linked equipment requirements and item values are updated to match the new skill requirements and combat rules.
  • Existing weapon damage, weapon speed, elemental blueprint bonuses, and resistance data will be updated where possible.
  • Armor, food, and droid resistance bonuses are being adjusted for the new Resistance system.
  • Droid CPU items and droid assembly options use the new combat values.
  • Some enhancement and food recipes now use Combat Readiness instead of the old cooldown-reduction bonus.
  • The update will adjust old saved items in one cleaner pass, so weapons, armor, enhancements, droids, storage, markets, ships, and outfits are handled more consistently.
  • Some enemies will now more clearly favor certain damage types or weaknesses, including Ice-themed Hutlar enemies, Electrical-themed CZ220 enemies, and Disruption-themed Korriban enemies.
  • New enemy variants include Coolant-Scarred Mynock, Byysk Cryo Adept, and Sith Frostbinder.

Building And Upgrading Lightsabers

  • Force Sensitive characters can now build their own lightsabers without a DM crafting the weapon. A DM only grants you a Kyber Token; everything after that is in your hands.
  • Use the token to attune it into a Kyber Token in your currency list, then visit a Lightsaber Workbench at the Sith Academy on Korriban, the Jedi Enclave on Dantooine, or a hidden cave on Dathomir.
  • At the workbench you build a tier 1 lightsaber or saberstaff: pick a hilt style and blade color, optionally socket up to two enhancements, and optionally apply a Weapon Submission Token to carry crafted stats onto the saber. Building consumes one Kyber Token.
  • Sabers grow stronger through tiered upgrade kits crafted with Engineering. Each kit raises the weapon one tier and keeps its existing bonuses, with the top "Chiro" tier requiring research to unlock.
  • Old DM-built sabers are normalized to a tier 5 baseline so the new upgrade kits work on them.

Quest Contracts

  • You can now post your own quests for other players at a Contract Board, backed by rewards you lock in up front.
  • Open a board to Browse and accept others' contracts or manage your own. To create one, set a title and description, add one to three item-delivery objectives by searching for items, and set the reward.
  • Publishing charges the reward credits, which are held in escrow, plus a small non-refundable posting fee. The escrow is paid to the first player who turns the contract in, or refunded to you if you cancel it or it expires after 30 days.
  • Completers accept a contract, gather the requested items, and turn it in at any board. Only the first player to finish earns the reward.
  • Rewards and returned items are handed out through a Claim Deliveries mailbox at any board, so nothing is lost to a full inventory. You can hold up to three active contracts at once.

Other Changes

  • The character name system has clearer unknown-character names.
  • Players can use /name on themselves to set the description other players see before they learn the character's name.
  • Settings now include more privacy controls for descriptor display and account-name visibility.
  • Disguise and persona support includes saved identities and matching display-name handling.
  • The Perks window has been redesigned to read at a glance: rows are color-coded by state (buyable, too expensive, skill-locked, or maxed), show the next upgrade's SP cost and an icon for what gates each perk, and can be filtered by All, Owned, Can Buy, or Maxed. Your available SP is shown in the window title.
  • Interplanetary travel now uses scheduled shuttles instead of instant flight terminals. You buy a ticket at a starport, wait for the next departure, board the shuttle near the terminal, and ride an instanced shuttle to your destination. Fares scale with distance, and unboarded tickets can be refunded for the base fare.
  • Banks now share one global storage account, so anything you deposit at one bank can be withdrawn at any other. Standard bank storage holds 120 items.
  • A new Mini-Vitals overlay shows your Stamina and FP as compact bars on your portrait, or your ship's shield, hull, and capacitor in space. It is on by default and can be toggled in Settings.
  • Some older area, creature, item, and dialogue text is clearer and more consistent.
  • A new Dantooine quest is available.
  • Several old perk categories, old perk items, old status effects, and old item requirements are cleaned up as part of the rebuild path.

Not Included In This Update

  • Espionage is not part of this combat update.
  • Farming-only Agriculture perks, such as Crop Management-style mechanics, are not part of this combat update.
  • Engineering, Fabrication, Gathering, and Piloting are not being presented here as standalone skill revamps. Any related changes are limited to combat-linked items, recipes, equipment, or handling old stored items.
  • Old Heavy Armor and Light Armor perk trees are not returning.
  • The old Heavy Armor activation-time penalty is not part of the new design.
