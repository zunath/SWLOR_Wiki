Dice Rolls

Intrepid Ground Grid

A grid-based ground combat system for infantry, vehicles, walkers, solo deployments, and special actions. It uses d20 attacks against DCs and mastery-based bonuses.

At a Glance

Core Rules

  • HP represents a unit's ability to move and fight as a coordinated unit. It may represent injuries, morale, stamina, equipment loss, or casualties.
  • A unit at 0 HP is incapable of fighting for the rest of the event unless the DM/QM says otherwise.
  • Base attacks are 1d20 + applicable modifiers vs DC 8 unless modified. Meeting the DC hits.
  • Diagonals count as 1.5 squares, rounded down. One diagonal is range 1; two diagonals are range 3.
  • A single attack cannot destroy a full-health PC-led unit. A second hit may.
  • Subcapital ships use the current Intrepid Space Grid rules.

How to Roll

Attack: d20 + applicable modifier vs DC 8
Special Action: d20 + full relevant mastery ranks vs declared DC

Infantry

For infantry, the 4 HP includes the PC's personal HP. If the last HP is lost, the PC is out of action for one full turn or forced to retreat a DM/QM-set distance.

Ground Vehicles

Masteries

If a single PC has multiple applicable masteries, use the best applicable mastery for movement, attack, and defense. They do not stack.

Examples

  • A pilot with Piloting 2 and Engineering 4 in a fighter can move minimum 2 and maximum 5, hits a plain enemy on a d20 roll of 7 or higher, and is hit by a plain enemy on a roll of 10 or higher.
  • An infantry PC with Dueling Mastery IV has +2 attack and +2 defense as infantry. They need a 6 to hit an ordinary opponent, and an ordinary opponent needs a 10 to hit them.

Special Actions

Force powers, devices, and DM-issued gear may be used in place of an attack to attempt buffs, debuffs, area control, healing, repair, or similar effects. The DM/QM determines whether an action is possible and what it does. Masteries are not always required, but may be required for specific actions.

  • Battle Meditation: Requires at least one relevant mastery. Roll d20 + full mastery ranks against DC 10. On success, allies gain +1 attack from the PC's grid. Roll once. The effect lasts until the end of the event, until countered, or until the PC leaves the ship or is defeated. The PC may take other actions on later rounds.
  • Heal: Infantry targeting infantry only. Roll d20 + full First Aid or Healing mastery ranks. Auto-success heals 1 HP. DC 10 heals 2 HP. May heal only to two-thirds max HP, rounded down. Same grid only.
  • Repair: Roll with full Engineering, Devices, or Fabrication mastery ranks. Captains may repair normally; passengers may attempt repairs with lesser bonuses. Repairs cannot exceed two-thirds of base HP, rounded down. Auto-success repairs 1 HP. DC 10 repairs one-eighth base HP, rounded up. DC 15 repairs one-quarter base HP, rounded up. Works for ground and space vehicles.

Exemplars

Rank V mastery may grant a reroll once per attempt or round for the relevant action.

  • Battle Meditation V = re-roll any Battle Meditation failure to reach DC10
  • Force Sense V = re-roll any failed detection roll (in Grid combat)
  • Force Speed/Agility V = If solo on foot, Base move 0-4 (in Grid combat), ignore terrain restrictions so long as you can fit your avatar in the destination Grid. Also counts as combat Mastery.
  • Force Leap/Telekinesis/Alter Environment V = Ignore building restrictions (eg bunkers) and fortifications (eg mines) in the scenario. If solo, ignore terrain/movement restrictions, if you can fit your avatar in your destination Grid. Also counts as combat Mastery.
  • Devices/Slicing V = re-roll relevant Special Actions (not Repair)
  • First Aid/Force Heal V = re-roll any Heal failure to reach DC10
  • Engineering/Fabrication V = re-roll any Repair failure to reach DC10

Failure and Aftermath

At 0 HP, a unit is out for the rest of the event unless the DM/QM declares a different consequence. PC personal consequences must be declared before rolling if they extend beyond temporary scene defeat.

DM/QM Guidance

Hostile units and player factions may have special rules or special units, whether known in advance or revealed during play. The artillery walker rules were noted as subject to possible future nerfs; use the listed values unless the DM/QM declares an event variant before play starts.
