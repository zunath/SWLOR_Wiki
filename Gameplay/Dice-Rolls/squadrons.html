SWLOR Squadrons

A squadron-scale starfighter system for fast space combat, custom loadouts, systems, overdrives, and structure damage.

At a Glance

Best ForStarfighter missions, squadron fights, ace duels, and special space events.
Players NeededOne or more pilots; supports multi-crew craft and NPC allies.
Dice Usedd20 for attacks and contests; weapon damage dice; d6 for structure damage and terrain risks; d2 for some debuffs or special effects.
Typical StakesShip damage, structure damage, lost weapons/systems, retreat, mission success, or ship destruction.
Needs DM/QMYes.
Consent RequiredRequired for PvP, permanent ship loss, or lasting character consequences.

Character Creation

  1. Choose a specification. This determines ship stats and loadout slots.
  2. Choose weapons, systems, and one overdrive.
  3. Confirm any restricted, black-market, or mission-limited options with the DM/QM.

Round Structure

There is no initiative. Players and NPCs alternate turns, agreeing who acts until everyone has taken a turn. Every turn uses the same order.

  1. Protocols: Start-of-turn actions.
  2. Movement: Move your full Speed before taking actions other than protocols.
  3. Actions: You have 2 AP each turn. Systems or overdrives may grant extra AP, but you may spend a maximum of 4 AP per turn.
  4. Resolution: Resolve weapons or systems that trigger at the end of the turn without AP cost.

Terminology

TermMeaning
SpeedThe number of range bands you must move each turn. Speed is also added to Evasion.
HullYour ship's core durability. Hull damage can cause structure damage at 0.
ShieldFirst layer of defense. Damage removes shields before hull. Unless a weapon has Overkill, excess shield damage does not carry over to hull.
ArmorSecond layer of defense. Hull damage is reduced by Armor.
AttackThe number added to the d20 roll to beat the target's Evasion. Usually determined by weapon.
RangeDistance from the opponent. Range bands are Engaged, Close, Medium, Long, and Distant.
EvasionThe target number to hit the ship.
Hull DebuffsStatus effects targeting the ship's hull. They are typically difficult to remove.
Computer DebuffsStatus effects targeting the ship's computer. They can usually be removed with a system or power cycle.
Deployment ZoneThe mission area where your ship can begin.

How to Roll

Attack: d20 + weapon Attack bonus vs target Evasion
Damage: roll the weapon's damage die on a hit
Structure Damage: d6 when Hull reaches 0

Attack results over 20 are critical hits. On a critical hit, roll the damage dice twice and take the higher result.

Movement Restriction

At the start of your turn, move your full Speed between range bands. You cannot move back and forth between a square you previously occupied unless you use Try Spinning.

Structure Damage

When Hull reaches 0, roll d6. For each previous Structure Damage roll in the same engagement, roll one extra d6 and take the lowest die.

RollResult
6Hold True: return to full Hull.
5Close Call: restore 75% of Hull.
4Armor Failure: lose 1 Armor and restore 75% of Hull.
3System Destroyed: choose a system and lose it. Restore 50% of Hull.
2Weapon Destroyed: choose a weapon and lose it. Restore 50% of Hull.
1Catastrophic Failure: the ship is destroyed.

Special Mission Rules

  • Jousting: When facing an Ace, you may Joust. Joust is taken during Movement, costs 1 AP, and triggers when you enter Engaged range with an enemy Ace. Both sides make contested d20 rolls and add current Speed. The winner deals 2d8 damage, then continues movement.
  • Scanning: Some missions require short-range scanners. Scanning costs 2 AP unless modified.
  • Dangerous Terrain: If entering dangerous terrain, either lower Speed by 2 to navigate safely or risk it. If you risk it immediately, and at the start of each turn you remain there, roll d6. On 1, take the terrain penalty.
  • Asteroid Field: Dangerous terrain. On failure, take 2d6 Overkill damage.
  • Static Minefield: Dangerous terrain. On failure, take 1d8 damage.
  • Moving Battle: Some missions center on a moving object. At the start of each round, the object moves, but remains the center square. Players may immediately move with it by spending movement on their turn or be moved away from it.
  • Aces: Aces receive two extra stat points allocated among Hull, Armor, Shields, Speed, Evasion, Attack, Weapon Slots, and System Slots. When destroyed, an Ace rolls Structure Damage instead of leaving the fight immediately.
  • Reinforcements: Reinforcements appear in their side's deployment zone.
  • Restricted Equipment: Unless specified, only one restricted loadout option may be deployed across the whole flight team.
  • Buff Stacking: Buffs do not stack unless they last for only one round.
  • Combined Arms: Ground units act together at the end of the round. Ground units cannot target aerial units unless they have anti-air weapons. Aerial weapons have one less range band when targeting ground units, except bomber specification ships.
  • Allies: NPC allies join the player turn order and may act before players if the players choose.

Capital Ships

Capital ships are made of three components: bridge, engines, and guns. Each component has its own Hull, but all three share the same Shield.

ComponentDestroyed Effect

Failure and Aftermath

At 0 Hull, use Structure Damage. Catastrophic Failure destroys the ship unless the DM/QM declared a variant. Permanent asset loss or character consequences must be declared before the mission starts.

Clarifications Needed

  • Several phases in the system table are blank in the source sheet. Treat them as requiring DM/QM confirmation before play.
  • Aphelion and Kraata entries are incomplete or classified.
  • Basilisk Small Arms has no damage value listed.
  • Some Typhaeon d6 results are undefined.
  • The black-market Spiteful Droid lists 0 uses; confirm whether this means passive/unlimited or unavailable before play.
