Prime Campaign Dice

This page covers Prime-style campaign dice systems: general checks, exploration, small-scale combat, PvP warzones, sniper duels, and Duel of the Fates.

Consent and Stakes

The DM/QM declares the event difficulty, ruleset, stakes, and any special risks before play begins. Any scene involving PvP, capture, maiming, permadeath, or lasting consequences requires the appropriate player consent before rolling. Special high-risk options such as unusual stunts or Duel of the Fates must be explained before the roll.

General Rules

  • Most checks use d20 + one applicable mastery.
  • Each mastery rank adds +1, up to +5.
  • A player cannot use more than one mastery on the same roll unless the DM/QM directs otherwise.
  • d100 rolls are used for critical decisions and may be used for exhaustion checks if the DM/QM believes a character is doing too much in a short time.
  • How masteries work can differ between exploration, small-scale combat, and large-scale combat. Use the section for the active ruleset.

Wounds

Wounds act as HP. Unless the active system says otherwise, each PC starts with 3 wounds. Each damage instance removes one wound. Players are usually allowed to heal a wound once through Force healing or a medkit injection. Additional wounds may be offered by DM/QM discretion.

Dark Side Influence

Force-sensitive characters who are not already fully committed to the Dark Side may be privately prompted by the narrator after dark or evil choices. The benefits or consequences of accepting that path are revealed to characters who accept it.

High-Risk Stunts

If a player wants to attempt something outside the normal dice systems, the DM/QM may allow a high-risk stunt. These are usually resolved as a 50/50 chance of a very good or very poor outcome. Depending on the request, the risk can include permadeath even in an otherwise low-risk event. The DM/QM must explain the risk before the roll.

Small-Scale Combat

Small-scale PvE combat uses d20 rolls with mastery modifiers. It is intended for scenes in the range of roughly 1-8 participants.

  • Each PC starts with 12 AC and 3 wounds.
  • At 0 wounds, a PC is KO'd. Depending on event type, the DM/QM may call for death saving throws.
  • PCs have one free movement action. They may sacrifice their other action for more movement.
  • Moving away from an enemy may provoke an attack of opportunity.

Critical Results

  • Attack critical fail: If a player critically fails an attack, the enemy may roll d10. On 6 or higher, the enemy counters and damages the attacker. If the enemy also rolls 1 on that d10, the player gets a chance to counter.
  • Enemy critical fail: If an enemy rolls 1 on an attack, the target may roll d10. On 6 or higher, the target counters and gains a hit.
  • Critical attack: A natural 20 deals 2 wounds instead of 1.
  • Other criticals: Aid, control, and other non-attack categories can produce stronger or more harmful results at DM/QM discretion.

PvP Warzones

PvP warzones are large-scale, objective-based battlegrounds. Teams try to defeat the enemy team or control the most objectives. Winning teams may receive bonuses that can be used in later campaigns or sagas, subject to DM/QM approval.

Warzone Structure

  • Teams begin in designated starting zones.
  • Grid combat is active.
  • The tactical phase happens first. Teams assign characters to objectives and declare relevant buffs or debuffs.
  • Objectives are resolved in sequence until the battlefield is complete.
  • The team with the most objective successes wins.
  • If the match ties, the DM/QM may call for a special tiebreaker, such as leader versus leader.

Battlefield Rules

  • Mechanical combat is not used during roll-based warzone play.
  • Masteries may be used if they make sense and the DM/QM approves.
  • Most PvP warzone rolls use d100 unless the DM/QM requests otherwise.
  • Turns are handled in small group round-robin. The DM/QM selects groups of about 3-5 players to act.
  • Each turn includes movement and an action. Full movement is 3 squares. A dash moves 4 squares and ends the turn early.
  • When finished, type EOT in comms so the DM/QM can mark the turn complete.

Warzone Combat

  • Attacker and defender roll contested d100.
  • The higher roll wins. On attacker success, the target loses HP.
  • A roll of 1 or 100 can create a critical failure or critical success. These can equal 2 HP lost.
  • If both participants roll the same number, it is a parry. Both roll d2 until one rolls 1, determining who loses the exchange.
  • Ganging up is allowed unless the scene is a Duel of the Fates or sniper duel, but objectives remain the main goal.

Warzone HP and Rerolls

  • Default HP is 3.
  • Winning a contested attack or defense deals 1 damage.
  • Offensive masteries grant rerolls, stacking to a maximum of 4 unless the character innately has a rank 5.
  • Defensive masteries grant extra HP, stacking to a maximum of 4 unless the character innately has a rank 5.
  • Only one pool can be 5.
  • Crafting masteries offer no benefit.
  • At 0 HP, the player is downed.

Healing, Buffs, and Debuffs

  • Each player has one heal for the entire warzone.
  • A nearby healer with healing masteries may allocate heals to teammates. They can have up to 4 heals, or 5 if they have a rank 5 healing mastery.
  • Battle Meditation and similar buffs are used during the tactical phase. Relevant mastery ranks determine how many rerolls the selected objective team receives. Excess rerolls can be assigned to another objective team.
  • Tactics and Strategy grants allies +1 square range for ranged weapon damage.
  • Debuffs work like buffs but remove rerolls from the enemy. Choose an objective, use relevant masteries, and attempt the debuff. If the enemy rolls below 50, they lose one reroll.
  • Buffs and debuffs can cancel each other.
  • Paralysis may be attempted with an appropriate mastery and a successful d100 roll. The target may remove it with a successful d100 roll. Until removed, they cannot move or take actions. Paralysis does not stack.

Weapons and Objectives

  • Average weapons, including pistols, blasters, and cannons, have range 3 squares.
  • Scoped weapons such as sniper rifles have range 5 squares.
  • Melee weapons have range 1 square.
  • Explosives such as grenades, mines, rocket launchers, and wrist rockets have limited ammunition and may deal AOE damage.
  • Grenades, rocket launchers, and wrist rockets affect 4 squares forward and 2 across. Targets roll d100 to save, succeeding above 50. Other affected creatures roll d2 to see whether they are hit.
  • Mines mark a location with a red glow. A creature that steps on a mine square takes 1 HP damage.
  • Objectives are marked with orange shafts of light. They may require a specific mastery or simply require control.
  • Objectives take 3 rounds to capture and can be interrupted by enemies.
  • Focused objectives allow one operator at a time. Damage to the operator cancels their action, requiring a retry. Teammates one square away may take the hit for them; no more than two players can protect an operator at once.
  • If an objective goes beyond round 5, defenders may add +1 off-field teammate to the field. This is limited to 1 additional teammate per 2 rounds.

Sniper Duel

If both players have sniper rifles or sniping masteries, they may enter a sniper duel. Sniper duels use only d2 rolls.

  • 1 is a failure. 2 is a success.
  • If both players roll 1 or both roll 2, the attacks negate each other and the round ends with no HP loss.
  • Sniping masteries grant d2 rerolls, limited to a total of 5 depending on mastery count.
  • A sniper in a sniper duel cannot use overwatch until they defeat their opponent.
  • If both players raise the stakes, the duel may become a Duel of the Fates and grants 2 extra HP.

Duel of the Fates

Duel of the Fates is a high-stakes duel between two players. It directly affects the warzone and overall narrative, and it places permadeath risk on the table.

  • Both players must agree that permadeath is on the table before the duel is approved.
  • Both players receive equal HP and rerolls.
  • The duel happens apart from normal round setup and proceeds at the duelists' pace.
  • The duel cannot be interrupted without explicit DM/QM permission.
  • Where the duelists would have been on the battlefield, an NPC with comparable stats takes their place.
  • When one player reaches 0 HP, the winner determines the loser's fate. The default is permadeath, but capture or maiming are valid at the winner's discretion.
  • Winning a Duel of the Fates does not necessarily win the warzone, but it helps the winner's team by adding 4 elite forces, each with 3/3 stats, to the match.
  • A Duel of the Fates may grant rare or special rewards at DM/QM discretion.

Failure and Aftermath

Use the declared event difficulty and stakes. In small-scale combat, 0 wounds usually means KO and may lead to death saves if announced. In warzones, 0 HP means downed unless a special phase declares higher stakes. In Duel of the Fates, 0 HP means the winner chooses the pre-consented high-stakes outcome.

DM/QM Guidance

Declare difficulty, risk tier, applicable masteries, objective rules, turn pacing, tie handling, special phases, and high-risk stunt consequences before rolling. Keep PvP warzone objectives clear enough that players know whether a personal win affects only their duel or the larger battlefield.
