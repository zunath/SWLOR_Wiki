Intrepid Quick Event Combat

This is a lightweight ground event system for quick tactical scenes where the DM/QM wants simple movement, HP, damage, and damage reduction.

At a Glance

Setup

  • Each PC has 25 HP for this event format.
  • Each player should know their best offensive mastery and best defensive mastery before play begins (if any. Don't worry, brand-new characters can still achieve goals)
  • A single mastery cannot count as both offensive and defensive.
  • Movement is measured in event squares, not normal grid-combat facing. The DM/QM will show the map scale in play.

Movement

  • A character may move 0-4 squares. Movement is orthogonal, ie you can leave a square in any direction EXCEPT diagonally.
  • A character may withdraw from a square containing active ground hostiles.
  • A character may not move through a square containing active ground hostiles.

How to Roll

  • To attack: Specify the target, roll d20, then state the final roll and applicable bonuses in party chat before emoting the attack.
  • Your character must have line-of-sight to the target at the moment of the attack. Exception: Devices mastery slotted as offense.
  • A character can conduct a single attack at any stage during their move. Ie they can move, attack, then move the rest of their allowed amount. 
  • They can target any opponent in same square or adjacent orthogonally (not diagonally). 
  • If you have a ranged weapon in hand, or Devices/Lightning/TK/Throw mastery slotted in offense, they can attack at upto range 3, measured orthogonally. Line-of-sight restriction remains (except for Devices)
Target: [target name]
Roll: d20
Party chat: [roll] + [bonus] = [total]

Damage and Defense

  • The best offensive mastery is added directly to the attack result and is subtracted from the hostile target's HP.
  • The best defensive mastery is subtracted from incoming damage as direct mastery DR.
  • One other form of DR may be added to mastery DR. Eg shield plus kneeling applies only one relevant DR.
  • NPC's will have hp totals displayed in-game. They will typically roll 1d10 for damage. Named Elites will typically roll 1d20 and are much more dangerous.

Special DR Rules

  • A shield in hand grants +2 DR to yourself.
  • A lightsaber in hand grants +2 DR against blaster fire to yourself and friendlies behind you.
  • Kneeling behind plausible cover grants +2 DR to yourself from attacks that pass over cover.

Failure and Aftermath

Characters reduced to 0 HP are out of the scene or suffer the DM/QM-declared event consequence. Any lasting outcomes will be declared by DM/QM before event start.

Special Actions

  • Damaging actions are represented by attack, for sake of this being a Quick ruleset. A PC can attempt an action that does not inflict damage on a target in place of Attack.
  • Example: Heal. Roll 1d10 (+any relevant Healing mastery ranks), restores that amount of health to one PC, including recovering from 0 HP if allowed by DM/QM for that event.
