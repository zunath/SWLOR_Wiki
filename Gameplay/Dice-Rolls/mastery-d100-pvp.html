Mastery d100 PvP

A simple contested d100 system for RP combat where one side attacks, the other defends, and the higher roll wins the exchange.

At a Glance

Best ForSimple PvP or contested RP fights.
Players NeededTwo or more.
Dice Usedd100.
Typical StakesDefeat, retreat, capture, or other declared PvP consequences.
Needs DM/QMRequired for high stakes or when PvP rules require it.
Consent RequiredYes. The system and stakes must be agreed before rolling.

Consent and Stakes

Use this system only after all participants know the stakes. Capture, mutilation, permadeath, or lasting consequences require the victim's OOC consent under the PvP rules. Dice results are binding once the system and stakes are agreed and rolling begins.

Setup

  • Each character starts with 3 HP.
  • Each character chooses applicable offensive masteries and defensive masteries before the first roll.
  • Offensive masteries grant rerolls for the fight. Add the applicable offensive mastery ranks together, to a maximum of 5 rerolls.
  • Defensive masteries grant extra HP. Add the applicable defensive mastery ranks together, to a maximum of 5 extra HP.
  • Only one pool may reach 5. If one side of the build reaches 5, the other pool cannot also be treated as 5 for this system.
  • Crafting masteries do not count.

How to Roll

Use the dice bag or the agreed d100 roll option. State whether the roll is an attack or defense roll before rolling.

Attacker: d100 attack
Defender: d100 defense

Roll Procedure

  1. The attacker declares the target.
  2. The attacker rolls d100.
  3. The defender rolls d100.
  4. The higher roll wins the exchange.
  5. The winner deals 1 damage. This can happen on either attack or defense.
  6. A participant may spend one available reroll when the system allows their side to reroll.
  7. When a character reaches 0 HP, they are defeated for the agreed stakes.

Example

A character has Ataru 4 and Telekinesis 1 as offensive masteries, plus Endure Pain 3 and Force Sense 1 as defensive masteries. They start with 5 rerolls and 7 HP: 3 base HP plus 4 defensive mastery HP.

Failure and Aftermath

At 0 HP, the defeated character suffers the pre-declared outcome. Do not add capture, maiming, permadeath, or other lasting consequences after the dice have already started unless all affected players agree and DM/QM approval is obtained when required.

Setup Template

Clarifications Needed

  • The source system does not define tie handling. Participants or the DM/QM must declare how ties work before rolling.
