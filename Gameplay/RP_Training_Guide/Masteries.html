Masteries

Masteries are DM-granted ranks that represent specialized training, practice, and expertise beyond normal game skills. They are primarily for roleplay, character identity, and DM-event context.

Masteries have no guaranteed mechanical benefit. Some DMs may use mastery ranks when calling for event skill checks, but use is case-by-case. Take masteries for RP and flavor, not for a promised combat or system advantage.

At a Glance

Training Time

Training time represents the character practicing and developing the skill in roleplay. Higher ranks require stronger roleplay support, and each rank needs its own justification.

Changing Masteries

  • You may reallocate progress from one mastery to another with appropriate roleplay and a ticket update.
  • You may remove a mastery. Removing a mastery opens a new 14-day training slot.
  • If the removed mastery used a quick slot, that quick slot is returned.

Request Format

Submit a ticket once the character is eligible. Use this format:

Mastery Request
Character Name:
Character Age:
Masteries Requested:
RP Justification for Each Mastery or Rank-Up:
  • Character Age: only required the first time the character requests masteries. Include a screenshot from the /char command showing the character is over 14 days old.
  • Masteries Requested: list up to 3 masteries in the order you want them queued, including rank. Example: Piloting 1, Piloting 2, Diplomacy 1.
  • RP Justification: explain how the character has used, trained, or developed the skill in roleplay. Screenshots or logs are helpful but not required.

Justifications should focus on in-game roleplay rather than long backstory. Staff may deny requests that do not show enough active roleplay for the requested rank.

Special Cases

Miscellaneous Masteries

You may request a mastery that is not listed below, especially for RP-focused skills. The list is not exhaustive, but unlisted masteries are less likely to be approved.

Dark Side and Corruption

Dark Side masteries must be portrayed as corruptive and dangerous. Powerful Dark Side masteries, including Sorcery, Alchemy, Mechu-Deru, and Necromancy, require appropriate roleplay around corruption, cost, and consequence. Characters cannot pursue or use the Dark Side as an objectively good or harmless path.

High-rank Dark Side practitioners are assumed to have practiced extensively. Consequences may be temporary or permanent depending on the character's actions and the story involved.

Rarity

Rare masteries are restricted and handled case by case. Rare approval is never guaranteed.
