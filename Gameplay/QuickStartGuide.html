Character Creation

Pick your Archetype 

Before you get started, you'll want to figure out what kind of character you want to play. Force Sensitivity, or your character's ability to access the Force is determined at character creation. Force Sensitivity is necessary for playing a lightsaber-wielding, force-magic using character, while Standard characters have access to a broader range of tech-skills, like Devices and First Aid, as well as Weapon Skills that Force Sensitives do not get. For more information regarding builds, you can read the Character Builds Guide.

Character Builds Guide

 

 

The Character Sheet

The character sheet in SWLOR is different than what you will find in other NWN servers. By pressing C you will open a large black box which can be freely resized and moved around the screen. 

  1. Character's Name
  2. Portrait
  3. Change Portrait  (Allows you to change your own portrait at any time)
  4. Archetype (Force Sensitive or Standard)
  5. Current/Max Health Points (In red), Current/Max Force Points (n blue), Current/Max Stamina (in green)
  6. Accrued/Max Skill Points and Attribute Points
  7. Character Attributes (Might, Perception, Vitality, Willpower, Agility, and Social)
  8. Character race (mechanically speaking, since there are many playable races not represented in character creation)
  9. A readout of your current Main Hand Damage and your current Off Hand Damage (determined by equipped weapons)
     
  10. Attack (dictates the amount of damage dealt in addition to your weapon's physical damage and your weapon's primary damage stat.) 
    Accuracy (dictates your chances of landing attacks.) 
    Evasion (dictates how difficult you are to hit for enemies.) 
     
  11. Phys. DEF (dictates how much damage you take from physical sources.) 
    Force DEF (dictates how much damage you take from Force sources.) 
    Elem. DEF (dictates how much damage you take from elemental sources.) 
     
  12. Control (dictates how much quality progress you make in crafting.) 
    Craftsmanship (dictates how much creation progress you make in crafting.) 
     
  13. Sav. Throws (Dictates your saving throws against skills and powers, from left to right they are Fortitude, Reflex, and Will).
     
  14. Side Bar-  Has various buttons. From top to bottom, they are
  • Skills - (Lists all of your skills in the game and allows you to lock a skill from degrading, as well as allowing you to spend RPXP on skills and languages.)
  • Perks - (Lists all of the perks in the game, allowing you to spend your SP on various perks to learn new abilities or gain access to various gear.)
  • Quests - A journal of sorts to keep track of all NPC quests and Guild requests.)
  • Appearance - (Allows you to customize the visuals of your character, weapons, armor, helmet, and cloak to personalize as you wish.)
  • Recipes - (Lists every recipe available to craft in the game, as well as the requirements to craft them.)
  • HoloCom - (Allows you to make or answer HoloCom calls to other players. Remember to /endcall to make sure the call ends.)
  • Key Items - (Lists key item flags tied to your character that do not show up in your inventory, such as keys and access cards.)
  • Currencies - (Lists your various refund tokens.)
  • Achievement - (List of achievements in the game, such as killing x enemies, crafting x items, or exploring certain areas and completing quests.)
  • Notes - (Lets you keep various notes. Multiple separate note entries can be made, allowing for organization.)
  • Open Trash - (Opens a trade window with an invisible NPC that will delete any items you leave in the inventory at the top, permanently. Use cautiously.)
  • Settings - (Allows you to toggle whether you are notified of achievements, toggle holonet visibility, Turn on subdual mode to prevent killing other players when reducing them to 0 HP, toggle visibility of hourly reset reminders, toggle whether you gain Force XP from melee attacks with lightsabers and electroblades.)

 

The Setting

Current Timeline

SWLOR is set in the year 3956 BBY in the Legends timeline of the Star Wars universe, currently experiencing the events of KOTOR 1. The main community hub and planet for roleplay is Viscara, a small planet in the outer rim that was once home to some ancient Sith that are now nothing more than ruins and crumbling architecture. Viscara is also a Force Nexus, allowing for a high concentration of Force Sensitives gathering in one place.

 

Factions

There are several playable factions available on the server.

NPC Factions: Factions ingrained into the setting that are driven by NPC leadership and DM's, as well as gaining access to fast track masteries.

  • The Viscaran Jedi Order
  • The Viscaran Revanite Sith Enclave
  • Republic Navy Fort Hope
  • The Mandalorians
  • The Hutt Cartel
  • Czerka Company
  • Malak's Sith Empire

Player Factions: Factions that are more player driven with less NPC and DM guidance or propulsion and no mastery fast tracks.

  • Smuggler
  • Redmoon Hall
  • Blue Diamond
  • Jal Shey
  • Worker's Respite Township
  • Gimpassa
  • Cadence
  • Celestial Phantom

 

Joining A Faction

All you have to do is approach a member of either faction in character, either through tracking them down In Game, or through the Holonet. Your character can start with a pre-existing history with the chosen faction, so long as it is as the lowest rank (i.e Jedi Initiate/Sith Aspirant/Republic Private). You can pursue in character to ascend the ranks and grow towards higher ranks.

Some factions, such as Jedi, Sith, and Mandalorians, acquire DM items at certain ranks. For Jedi/Sith these would take the form of Lightsabers, something your character will make when they are taken as a Padawan. For Mandalorians they receive Beskar with their promotion to Verd, instead. Beskar is a unique lightsaber-resistant armor that is incredibly durable.  

Sith/Jedi should log their training for the DMs to track their progress. To do so, open a ticket via the discord and post a short blurb explaining what your character learned/trained and with who. Written short-stories on the forums about your character training/practicing will also be considered.

Attaining the rank of Jedi Knight or Sith Inquisitor will take some time, so be sure to enjoy and invest plenty of your energy into the training and learning of your character as they work towards it. Lightsabers are treasured items in Star Wars, so be prepared to dedicate time toward being an initiate and working toward your lightsaber. But don't worry, your Jedi/Sith peers can make you a training foil that in the mean-time that will still signify your affiliation with a Force Order.

Quick Start Tutorial:

Upon entering the game your character will find themself in a shuttle heading to CZ-220, the landing point for star ships entering the planet Viscara. Here, you can edit your character's appearance by pressing C or opening the Character sheet, and then clicking “Appearance”. From the Settings button in the window, you can also adjust your character's height. Please be responsible with designing your character's look. Tomfoolery will be addressed by the DMs. 

Talk to the Twi'lek at the back of the ship to proceed into the tutorial zone, CZ-220.

Welcome to SWLOR!

After making your character you will spawn into the welcome area! There are various NPCs around who will talk to you about various mechanics present in the game, such as the pilot speaking about learning Piloting or the bounty hunter speaking about how engineers make droids. Others will speak on factions, like the Force sensitive civillian who speaks of the Jedi and Revanite Sith.

Make sure to stop at Sal Vulcano's shop (the Gamorrean on the left as you exit) to get some basic clothes and a helmet so you can set up your signature look before setting foot onto the server proper, and don't forget to save it to your outfits slots!

To change your outfits open the character sheet with C and click Appearance on the right.

  1. Appearance Tab - This allows you to customize your skin color, hair color, eye color, tattoo color, and body parts separate from your clothing.
  2. Equipment Tab - This allows you to customize your armor, helmet, cloak, main hand weapon, and off hand weapon appearances.
  3. Settings Tab - This includes the ability to toggle visibility of your helmet, cloak, and to increase or decrease your height.
  4. Color Target - This changes what is being colored accordingly, such as hair, skin or tattoo on Appearance, or Leather 1, Leather 2, Cloth 1, Cloth 2, Metal 1, and Metal 2 on Equipment.
  5. Part Target- This changes what part of your body you are changing, such as your head, hands, torso, and so on.
  6. Color Picker - This is where you select the color to be applied to your color target.
  7. Part Picker - This is where you pick the model to be applied to your part.
  8. Part Cycler - For quickly moving to the next model in the Part Picker list without having to scroll and click.

In the equipment tab you can select the drop down bar highlighted on the left to access the list of modifiable equipment. This will only apply to equipment currently equipped to your character. On the right is the Outfits tab, where you can store an save up to 25 outfits.

 

Once you've spoken to all the NPCs that interest you and set up your new outfit, speak to Captain S'luuk to be sent to CZ-220, the tutorial area of SWLOR.

 

CZ-220 and Selan's Request

After landing you will arrive in the hangar. Once you've spoken to the NPCs you wish to interact with here head straight ahead into the main section of CZ-220.

As soon as you enter you will be greeted by Selan Flembek, the NPC with your first quest! Speak to her and accept her quest to continue.

Note: If you have done Selan's quest once on your player handle, you won't need to do it again to leave the zone, though the credits may be valuable early on.

She will ask you to aid 3 of the NPCs around the space station:
 

  • Halron Linth - He will ask you to kill enemies in the lower levels. First mynocks, then droids, and finally a mini boss experiment creature.
  • Avix Tatham - He will ask you to gather ore, then ask you to refine it.
  • Crafting Terminal Droid Operator - They will task you with gathering items from crates and scrap piles and then with crafting, crafting a bedroll, or crafting a knife.

 

Each NPC has it's own quest to give you. You only need to do the first quest for each to satisfy Selena's quest. Once the quest is complete, the shuttle to Veles will be available. 

 

Other notable NPCs in this area include: 

  • Darryn Beto, by the Crafting Droid; who will sell you all of your starting gear and provide you with all the things you need to survive.
  • Dario Breto, by the front desk; who will sell Training Weapons. These are much weaker versions of their respective weapons, but require no perks to equip. Good for roleplay and for disguises!
  • Cheriss Ordo, just past the desk and Dario in the bar; who has a datapad you can retrieve from the experiment room and return to her for a reward.
  • Frank, in the cooking pots area; Will sell you roleplay prop items like cigars, cigarettes, cups, and goblets.

Once you've completed the requests and spoken to Selan to get your ticket, it's time to head to Viscara and enter the open world of SWLOR! Don't worry, you can come back to CZ any time.

 

Veles City Highlights

There is much to do in Veles, the main city of Viscara, owned and operated by Czerka Corporation. But there are some key quests you may wish to do. Be warned, some of these should not be addressed immediately unless you have help, so feel free to level up a bit or ask around for someone to help you with these (particularly the mandalorian facility).

  • Taxi Terminal Repairs - Given by Dessta Bocktorb, a blue twi'lek directly to your right after you exit the Starport. This will unlock the taxi system and let you get around Veles much easier.
  • Kath Hound Part Collection - Given by Szaan Synko just in front of the Starport, by the central square.
  • Kath Hound Hunting - Given by Moira Halaz, just outside of the central square headed south, towards the exit to Veles.
  • Weapons for Krystalle - Given by Krystalle in the far north-eastern area, in the refugee section in a back alleyway.
  • Package for Denam Reyholm - Given by Roy Moss in the same alley as Krystalle, unlocks the ability to respec your character. This quest will take you into the mountains which is a bit harder, so make sure you can handle the area first.
  • Locate the Mandalorian Facility - Given by Tal'gar Meyne by the market terminals directly north of the central square, this quest chain unlocks a fairly important spot to grind for lower levels before leaving the planet. That being said, you should wait until you are struggling to grind from the rest of the planet to go here, and may still wish to bring a friend.

 

After completing some of these quests and their follow ups you should check in with Irene Colstaad, north of the market terminal (just in front of Czerka tower), and Harry Mason, whom can be found right next to Roy Moss.
